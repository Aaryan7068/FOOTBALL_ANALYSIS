Step 1: Player & Ball Tracking

The process begins by feeding each video frame into a pre-trained **YOLO (You Only Look Once)** model. This powerful deep learning model detects all players and the ball, drawing a bounding box around each.

The `Tracker` class then assigns a persistent ID to each detected object, allowing the system to follow their movements across the entire video. To optimize performance, these tracking results are cached in a `tracks_stubs.pkl` file, saving significant processing time on subsequent runs.

Step 2: Camera Movement Estimation

Broadcast cameras are never static; they pan, tilt, and zoom to follow the action. To accurately measure player movement on the pitch, we must first compensate for the camera's own movement. The `CameraMovementEstimator` analyzes the optical flow between frames to calculate the camera's motion vector. This vector is then subtracted from each player's tracked position, isolating their true movement on the field.

Step 3: Tactical View Transformation

The standard broadcast view is not suitable for tactical analysis. The `ViewTransformer` module performs a **perspective transformation**, mathematically converting player positions from the angled camera view to a flat, 2D "bird's-eye" view. This step is crucial for accurately calculating real-world distances between players and understanding team formations.

Step 4: Data Enrichment & Analytics

With accurate, real-world coordinates, the system enriches the data with high-level analytics. This is where raw tracking data becomes meaningful insight.

Team Assignment & Possession

A K-Means algorithm clusters jersey colors to assign teams. Possession is given to the player closest to the ball.

Player Performance Metrics

Speed and distance are calculated from the transformed coordinates, providing key performance data.
